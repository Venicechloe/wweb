RNHS has a long-standing tradition of academic excellence and community involvement. From its humble beginnings, it has grown into a thriving institution, nurturing generations of learners and leaders.

OUR HISTORY

Rosales National High School is one of the oldest public secondary schools in the municipality of Rosales, Pangasinan, Philippines. It was established to provide accessible secondary education to students in Rosales and nearby towns, especially at a time when only a few high schools existed in the area. On June 19, 1965, the school was officially converted into a national high school through Republic Act No. 4325. This law allowed the school to receive government support for facilities, teachers, and educational programs. Because of this, the school continued to grow and accommodate more students from different communities in eastern Pangasinan. Over the years, Rosales National High School has expanded its buildings, academic programs, and student organizations. Today, it continues to serve thousands of students and plays an important role in shaping the education and future of the youth in Rosales and nearby municipalities.
